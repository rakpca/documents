# Display of Religious Items Policy

**Date Approved:** March 27, 2022  
**Certified By:** Joseph Mazzarella, President/Secretary

### Policy Overview
Owners and residents are generally permitted to display or affix religious items on their property or dwelling if motivated by sincere religious belief.

### Rules and Regulations
**1. ACC Approval:**
* **Requirement:** An Architectural Control Committee ("ACC") application must be submitted and approved in writing before installation.
* **Submission Content:** Must include the type/description of the display and a site plan indicating location relative to building lines, rights-of-way, setbacks, or easements.

**2. Exemptions from ACC Approval:**
* **Entryway Items:** Items displayed on the entry of a dwelling do not require approval if they do not exceed twenty-five (25) square inches.
* **Seasonal Decorations:** Temporary seasonal religious holiday decorations may be displayed no more than 60 days before and 30 days after the holiday. The Board has sole discretion to determine what constitutes a seasonal holiday decoration.

**3. Prohibitions:**
A religious item display is prohibited if it:
* Threatens public health or safety.
* Violates a law other than one prohibiting religious speech.
* Contains language or graphics patently offensive to a passerby for reasons other than religious content.
* Is installed on property owned/maintained by the Association or common areas.
* Violates any building line, right-of-way, setback, or easement.
* Is attached to a traffic control device, street lamp, fire hydrant, or utility pole/fixture.

# 209 Hearing Policy

**Date Approved:** March 27, 2022  
**Statutory Basis:** Texas Property Code Section 209.007

### Hearing Parameters
**I. Definitions:**
* **ACC:** Architectural Control Committee. A person may not serve on the ACC if they are a current board member, a board member's spouse, or reside in a board member's household (unless during the development period).

**II. General Rules:**
* **Scheduling:** Hearings must be held no later than 30 days after the request is received. Postponements of up to 10 days may be granted upon request.
* **Notice:** A Hearing Notice with date, time, and location (or virtual access) must be sent at least 10 days prior.
* **Evidence:** Owners must provide copies of documentary evidence to the Board no later than 5 days before the hearing.
* **Decision:** The Board is not required to make a decision during the hearing but may provide a written decision within 30 days.
* **Conduct:** The Board may limit time and terminate hearings that become unproductive or contentious.
* **Recording:** Either party may make an audio recording.

**III. ACC Denial Hearings:**
* **Appeal:** An ACC denial for improvements may be appealed to the Board.
* **ACC Notice:** Must describe the basis for denial and inform the Owner of the right to request a hearing within 30 days.
* **Outcome:** The Board may affirm, modify, or reverse the ACC decision.

**IV. Other Hearings (Fines, Suspensions, Damage Charges):**
* **Hearing Packet:** The Board must provide a packet containing all relevant documents, photos, and communications it intends to introduce.
* **Packet Deadline:** If the packet is not provided at least 10 days before the hearing, the Owner is entitled to an automatic 15-day postponement.
* **Order of Presentation:** The Association presents its case first, followed by the Owner.

# Security Measures Policy

**Date Approved:** March 27, 2022  
**Statutory Basis:** Texas Property Code Section 202.023

### Guidelines
**1. Approval:**
* An ACC application is required before constructing any security measure.
* Applications must include type, location, purpose, and construction/site plans.

**2. Fencing Requirements:**
* **Prohibited Materials:** Chain link, brick, concrete, barbed wire, electrified, vinyl, and stone are expressly prohibited.
* **Decorations:** No decorative elements such as finials, fleur de lis, or spears are allowed.
* **Vegetation:** No vines allowed on security fencing.
* **Placement:** Must not block or enclose sidewalks.

**3. Fencing Forward of Residential Structure:**
* **Material:** Must be metal (steel, wrought iron, aluminum) and max 6 feet tall.
* **Style:** Must have the appearance of "wrought iron fencing". Stamped metal, metal panels, and solid metal fencing are prohibited.
* **Design:** Straight horizontal rails and vertical pickets. Pickets 4 inches on center. Framing must be on the inside.
* **Color:** Must be black or ACC-approved color.
* **Transitions:** Cannot attach to wood fences; must terminate with a 3-inch metal post.

**4. Special Locations:**
* **Corner Lots:** No security fencing in the "Visibility Triangle" (15 feet from the intersection along the right-of-way).
* **Shared Lots:** If fencing is on a shared lot line, all Owners of Affected Lots must sign the ACC application.

**5. Burglar Bars and Enclosures:**
* Must be black or ACC-approved color.
* Must consist of straight horizontal rails and vertical pickets.
* Decorative elements are prohibited.

# Bid Solicitation Policy

**Date Approved:** March 27, 2022  
**Statutory Basis:** Texas Property Code Section 209.0052(c)

### Policy Details
**1. Applicability:**
* Applies to contracts for services (e.g., landscaping, pool maintenance, legal) exceeding **$50,000** over the contract term.
* Does not apply to contracts under $50,000, even if automatic renewals eventually exceed that amount.

**2. Procedure:**
* **Solicitation:** The Board must solicit multiple bids if available. This can be done via invitation to bid, RFP, or other methods.
* **Evaluation:** The Board determines criteria (price, insurance, references, etc.). The Board is not obligated to select the lowest bid.
* **Frequency:** For ongoing services (like landscaping), the Association must follow this process at least 3 months prior to the expiration of the current contract.
